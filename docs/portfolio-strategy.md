# Portfolio Strategy

## Target Reader

Anton Sten is not looking for a decorative portfolio. He is likely evaluating whether Hardik can be useful around early product work, prototyping, research synthesis, AI workflows, and technical follow-through.

## Positioning

Present Hardik as a product-minded operator who can help a design-led team move from ambiguity to a clearer working artifact.

Do not position Hardik as:

- a senior visual designer
- a polished brand designer
- a formal end-to-end product design owner

## What The Portfolio Must Signal

- good judgment around messy product problems
- comfort working near design, product, engineering, and operations
- strong written thinking
- real evidence of shipping systems, prototypes, and workflow improvements
- AI as a multiplier for execution, not as a substitute for product thinking

## Narrative Arc

1. Hardik works well where the path is not fully defined.
2. He turns messy constraints into prototypes, systems, or clearer operating decisions.
3. He can contribute to design-adjacent work without overstating formal design authority.
4. He is fast, technical, and practical enough to be useful on overflow or experimental work.

## Recommended Site Structure

1. Hero
2. Short positioning statement
3. Selected evidence / case studies
4. Working style and capabilities
5. Brief note on why this portfolio exists
6. Contact / links

## Recommended Case Study Order

1. SwimDrinkFish
Why: strongest proof of ambiguity handling, systems thinking, and cross-functional coordination.

2. PointClickCare
Why: strongest proof of product-adjacent execution, workflow thinking, and collaboration with engineering delivery.

3. Emotional Echoes
Why: strongest proof of product framing, AI intuition, and proactive concept thinking.

4. Picture This! or LoblawTank
Why: useful support evidence for product narrative, recommendation quality, and presentation.

## Tone

- direct
- thoughtful
- not over-designed
- precise about contribution
- confident without inflation
